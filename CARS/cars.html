CARS

WELCOME TO THE CARS SECTION.

1. CAR BASICS

Engine: The “heart” of the car, converts fuel into motion. Types include petrol (gasoline), diesel, electric, and hybrids (mix of fuel + electric).

Transmission: Transfers power from the engine to the wheels. Can be manual (gears you shift yourself) or automatic.

Drivetrain: Determines how the wheels get power.

FWD (Front-Wheel Drive) → efficient, cheaper cars.

RWD (Rear-Wheel Drive) → sporty, performance cars.

AWD/4WD (All-Wheel Drive/4x4) → off-road and all-weather stability.


2. TYPE OF CARS

Sedan: 4-door family cars (Toyota Camry, Mercedes S-Class).

Hatchback: Compact, with a rear door that swings upward (VW Golf, Honda Fit).

SUV: Bigger, higher ground clearance (Range Rover, Toyota Land Cruiser).

Truck/Pickup: Built for hauling and toughness (Ford F-150, Toyota Hilux).

Sports Car: Designed for speed & handling (Porsche 911, Nissan GT-R).

Supercar/Hypercar: Extreme performance (Bugatti Chiron, McLaren P1).

Electric Cars (EVs): Run on batteries (Tesla, Nissan Leaf).


3. PERFORMANCE TERMS

Horsepower (HP): Engine power, higher = faster acceleration.

Torque (Nm or lb-ft): Pulling power, important in trucks/SUVs.

0–100 km/h (0–60 mph): How fast it accelerates.

Top Speed: Max speed it can reach.


4. CAR BRANDS (Examples)

Luxury/Performance: BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Lexus.

Reliable Everyday: Toyota, Honda, Hyundai, Nissan.

American Muscle: Ford Mustang, Dodge Charger, Chevy Camaro.

Supercars: Ferrari, Lamborghini, McLaren, Bugatti.

Electric Leaders: Tesla, BYD, Rivian, Lucid.


5. FUTURE OF CARS

Electric Vehicles (EVs) are taking over (Tesla, BYD, Porsche Taycan).

Hybrid Cars combine engine + electric (Toyota Prius, Lexus RX).

Autonomous Cars (self-driving tech) are in testing (Tesla Autopilot, Waymo).
